Meet cite and details tags

Cite tag

The <cite> tag defines the title of a creative work (e.g. a book, a poem, a song, a movie, a painting, a sculpture, etc.).

Let's take a look at example 1

Mona Lisa by Leonardo da Vinci. Painted in 1503.

As you can see, the name of the painting is different than the other words.

Code example:

  <p>
    <cite>Mona Lisa</cite>
    by Leonardo da Vinci. Painted in 1503.
  </p>

Details tag

The <details> tag specifies additional details that the user can open and close on demand. The <details> tag is often used to create an interactive widget that the user can open and close. By default, the widget is closed. When open, it expands, and displays the content within.

Let's take a look at example 1

Click on WoW DoP below.

WoW DoP

WoW DoP is the best place where you can start from scratch and learn the coding way to become a successfull web developer. Join the program and watch your life change in just a a matter of a few months

Code example:

	<details>
		<summary>WoW DoP</summary>

		<p>
			WoW DoP is the best place where you can start from scratch and learn the
			coding way to become a successfull web developer. Join the program and watch
			your life change in just a a matter of a few months
		</p>
	</details>

Let's take a look at example 2

If we take a look at the example below, I have added the open attribute so that the details section is already open.

*open is a boolean, therefore it has only two values: 'true' and 'false'.
Moving to London

John decided to change his life and move to London to follow his dream of becoming the most successfull web developer.

Code example:

<details open>
	<summary>Moving to London</summary>

	<p>
		John decided to change his life and move to London to follow his dream of becoming the most successfull web developer.
	</p>
</details>
